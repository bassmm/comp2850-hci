# Interview Notes — Week 6

## Participant A
**Date**: 2025-11-23 12:50  
**Context**: Highly conscientious, engineering student, prefers structure, avoids social media distractions  
**Consent**: ✅ Confirmed verbally  
**Duration**: ~10 minutes

---

### Q1: Last time using task manager?
**Response**: "I use Todoist every morning to plan my day. I usually create subtasks for bigger projects and check them off as I go."

**Observations**:
- Prefers structured, step-based breakdowns
- Uses consistent routines

**Themes**: `structure`, `routine`, `breakdown`

---

### Q2: Biggest frustration?
**Response**: "When I can't reorder tasks on mobile or if a subtask disappears when I check off its parent."

**Observations**:
- Mobile app limitations are a blocker
- Concerned about accidental loss of information

**Themes**: `mobile_limitations`, `data_loss_anxiety`

---

### Q3: Lost track of important task?
**Response**: "Once, a subtask vanished after marking the parent complete. I spent 15 minutes trying to find it in the archive."

**Observations**:
- Subtask-parent relationship causes confusion
- Time wasted in recovery

**Themes**: `visibility`, `parent_child_confusion`

---

### Q4: Work without a mouse?
**Response**: "Mostly fine—shortcuts are important. But some icons have no keyboard equivalents so I use the mouse anyway."

**Observations**:
- Keyboard navigation is crucial for efficiency
- Keyboard-only workflow sometimes interrupted

**Themes**: `keyboard_nav`, `efficiency`

---

### Q5: Eyes closed / bright sunlight?
**Response**: "Bright sunlight is a problem. Light themes glare too much; I always use dark mode."

**Observations**:
- Environment-driven preference for dark UI
- Needs readable, non-glaring interface

**Themes**: `dark_mode`, `environmental_needs`

---

### Q6: One feature to add?
**Response**: "A timeline view to visualize project milestones and dependencies would really help."

**Observations**:
- Wants project management tools, not just lists
- Seeks high-level overviews

**Themes**: `timeline_view`, `project_tracking`

---

## Summary (Participant A)
**Top pain points**:
1. Mobile limitations and subtask disappearance → anxiety about task integrity and platform trust
2. Keyboard gaps interrupt efficiency for structured users

**Job story ideas**:
- "When I work on projects, I want a timeline view so I can see dependencies and deadlines clearly."
- "When I check off a task, I want subtasks preserved so accidental loss doesn’t happen."
- "When I’m on mobile, I want feature parity so I’m not blocked when away from my laptop."

## Participant C

**Consent**: ✅ Confirmed verbally  
**Duration**: ~10 minutes

---

### Q1: Last time using task manager?
**Response**: "Yesterday! I used Microsoft To Do to break down an essay assignment, but ended up jumping between apps and forgot half my plans."

**Observations**:
- Frequently context-switches between applications
- Relies on digital reminders but misses steps due to attention lapses

**Themes**: `context_switching`, `attention_span`, `reminder_reliance`

---

### Q2: Biggest frustration?
**Response**: "Tasks get buried if I don’t mark them straight away. Then I lose track when I come back later."

**Observations**:
- Struggles with visibility for incomplete tasks
- Needs cues for returning to unfinished items

**Themes**: `task_visibility`, `recency`, `unfinished_work`

---

### Q3: Lost track of important task?
**Response**: "So often! If it’s not right at the top, I assume it’s done or forget it’s there."

**Observations**:
- Overly dependent on list order for recall
- Misses dated or less prominent items

**Themes**: `reordering`, `top_of_list`, `memory_support`

---

### Q4: Work without a mouse?
**Response**: "Mostly on my phone. I do everything by touch, but wish I could use voice more, like quick add from lock screen."

**Observations**:
- Touch interface preferred, but voice input desired
- Accessibility through alternative input methods

**Themes**: `voice_input`, `touch_ui`, `accessibility`

---

### Q5: Eyes closed / bright sunlight?
**Response**: "Sometimes I use voice notes when my hands are busy or when out walking, need it to work with headphones."

**Observations**:
- Prioritizes convenience and on-the-move input
- Audio cues and compatibility with wearable tech

**Themes**: `audio_notes`, `wearable_support`

---

### Q6: One feature to add?
**Response**: "A ‘nag me’ mode that keeps reminding me if something is overdue, even if I dismiss it."

**Observations**:
- Needs persistent motivation and support against procrastination
- Open to repeated, configurable alerts for urgent items

**Themes**: `persistent_alerts`, `anti_procrastination`, `personalized_nags`

---

## Summary (Participant C)
**Top pain points**:
1. Forgotten or hidden tasks due to context-switching and recency bias
2. Reliance on list position and lack of persistent reminders for overdue work

**Job story ideas**:
- "When I forget to act on a task, I want persistent, escalating reminders so I can't easily lose track, even if I dismiss them."
- "When I’m out and about, I want to quickly add tasks by voice so I don’t lose ideas or commitments while multitasking."
